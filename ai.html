--- layout: basic title: AI tips --- There's a lot of talk about how AI can be powerfully used in conjunction with people, rather than to replace people. What does that actually mean in practice? Here are some ways that I've found to use AI that I think contain the kernel of something worth sharing.

A meditation on mortality

I asked Claude to flesh out this striking statistic that I heard that over the next 70 years, 5 billion people will die. I did a bunch of napkin math based on this, pasted it to Claude, and asked it to double-check my napkin math against actual statistics. It did that and also whipped up this wonderful little widget.

Introspection

LLMs are good at tip-of-the-tongue queries, since they are good at filling holes or slightly expanding the frontiers of our awareness and knowledge. Aren’t our own “unknown unknowns” a bit like that? That is what led me to come up with the following prompt on new years eve of 2025. A profound exercise.
Unknown unknowns. We have many in our life. Things we are missing out on that we wouldn't even know that we would enjoy. Blind spots in how we interact with others. Beliefs that we hold that are limiting us, and nobody dares to tell us about them. Ask me some questions to respond to, interact with my responses by soliciting one more round of questions, and then use that to diagnose some possible directions to explore or improve my life.

Labor organizing

In Colorado, CWA 7799 helped pass an Unfair Labor Practices bill that extended various workplace protections to public sector workers. These Unfair Labor Practices could then be reported to the state, but the reporting process involved filling out a confusing form that required identifying the specific section of the law that was violated. I created a custom GPT (way back in ancient history, 2023) that helped fill out these forms for the user. [link to chatbot]
