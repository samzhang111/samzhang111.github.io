Sam Zhang's Homepage

I am a 4th year PhD candidate in applied mathematics at the University of Colorado at Boulder. I am supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship in science policy.

My research uses mathematics and computer science to study the human side of science. One big question that I ask is: what are the effects of social inequalities on science? I am also part of the computational social science and complex systems communities more broadly. My advisor is Aaron Clauset.

During my PhD, I was a (2x) research intern with the MSR NYC Computational Social Science group, a Data Science & Human Rights Fellow with HRDAG, and before my PhD, a Data Science for Social Good Fellow at the University of Chicago.

I am organizing to improve public higher education with my labor union, United Campus Workers Colorado, and currently serving my second term on the Executive Board of CWA 7799.

More about me here.
