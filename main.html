Sam Zhang's Homepage

I am an Assistant Professor of Statistics at the Vermont Complex Systems Institute, where I direct the Science and Humanity Lab.

I conduct research on the dynamics of science, inequality in academia, and the broader social impacts of data and networks. My collaborators and I develop and apply methods drawing on mathematics, network science, and data science.

Our work examines how institutions shape scientific careers, how biases and hierarchies propagate through systems, and how quantitative approaches can inform questions of human rights and policy.

You can reach me at sam.zhang@uvm.edu.
